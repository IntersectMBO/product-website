# Cardano Vision 2030: Charting Our Course to Impact

## Our Strategic Mandate for the Ecosystem

### Vision 2030: Our Defining Goal

By 2030, Cardano aims to achieve **$200 million in annual Layer 1 (L1)
transaction fees**, demonstrating robust network utility and a self-sustaining
economic foundation. This metric will be a primary indicator of our success,
reflecting widespread adoption and impactful real-world use.

### Pathways to Achievement: Strategic Imperatives

To realize our 2030 Vision, Cardano will focus on the following strategic
imperatives, informed by community input and market demands:

#### 1. Expanding Enterprise and Institutional Adoption

* **Addressing Security Barriers:** Acknowledge and mitigate the
    application-level security concerns that are a non-starter for major
    corporations. This requires developing and integrating advanced security
    solutions, including real-time and AI-driven threat detection and
    prevention mechanisms that can intercept interactions with wallets and
    validators. The goal is to create a secure environment for large
    corporations to interact with public blockchains without compromising
    their stringent security standards.
* **Facilitating Financial Integration:** Streamline the process for
    enterprises to manage treasury and invoicing on Cardano. This includes
    addressing accounting, auditing, and tax reporting requirements,
    potentially through tools that bridge blockchain data with traditional
    financial software. Support for stablecoin and real-world asset (RWA)
    tokenization liquidity use cases, while currently often favoring private
    blockchains due to cybersecurity, must be enabled securely and
    efficiently on Cardano for broad institutional engagement.
* **Simplifying Onboarding:** Develop comprehensive solutions for legal
    and financial onboarding barriers, engaging with accountants, auditors,
    and legal advisors to ensure compliance and ease of integration for
    traditional businesses.

#### 2. Fostering a Culture-First Approach and Localized Engagement

* **Integrating Cultural Understanding:** Embed a culture-first approach as a
    foundational layer in our product roadmap processes. This involves
    structured insight gathering on how users in diverse regions define trust,
    empowerment, and connection, informing local use case design, messaging,
    and onboarding strategies.
* **Empowering Local Partnerships:** Prioritize identifying and
    collaborating with key local individuals and partners who can facilitate
    understanding and communication within their respective cultures. This
    includes leveraging local organizers for workshops globally, ensuring a
    wider range of perspectives and insights into diverse market needs are
    captured.
* **Localized Communication:** Invest in professional interpreters who not
    only translate language but also understand the subject matter, ensuring
    correct contextual communication across diverse communities. Acknowledge and adapt to cultural nuances,
    including religious perspectives on cryptocurrency, to facilitate broader
    adoption.
* **Targeted Market Identification:** Focus on "low-hanging fruit" markets
    already embracing crypto, such as Argentina and Venezuela, due to their
    immediate need for blockchain solutions to solve real-world problems.
    Shift from broad regional labels like "Global South"
    to specific, data-driven targeting based on economic factors, population,
    or education levels.

#### 3. Enhancing Developer Onboarding and Education

* **Centralized Documentation & Learning:** Address fragmented developer
    documentation by creating centralized, community-led resources with
    examples, videos, and clear onboarding paths. Simplify the learning process for complex
    blockchain concepts, making it accessible even for beginners.
* **Youth & Academic Engagement:** Propose and implement pilot programs for
    blockchain education in schools, integrating it into existing curricula
    (e.g., computer science) to engage younger generations naturally. This can involve school clubs, gamified learning, and
    even running localized blockchain networks (like SanchoNet) within
    educational settings.
* **Hands-on Experience:** Promote initiatives like the FIU Code Crunch 305
    Hackathon and the ALBA Lab deployment to provide students and developers
    with practical experience in Cardano tooling (Aiken, Plutus, CIP-1694)
    and real-world problem-solving through blockchain and AI convergence.

#### 4. Abstracting Blockchain Complexity for Mass Adoption

* **Invisible Technology:** Ensure that for the vast majority of end-users,
    the underlying blockchain technology operates seamlessly and
    imperceptibly, functioning as a robust tool under the hood rather than
    a visible layer requiring specialized knowledge.
* **Intuitive User Experience (UX):** Prioritize the development of dApps
    and services with UX design that mirrors familiar web2 applications,
    removing barriers associated with wallets, transaction signing, and fee
    management. This includes enabling features like Babel Fees to allow
    payment in non-ADA native assets.
* **Developer Empowerment for Abstraction:** Provide developers with the tools
    and frameworks necessary to build applications that effectively abstract
    away blockchain complexities for their users, allowing them to focus
    on delivering value and functionality.

#### 5. Bolstering SPO Incentives and Decentralization

* **Diversifying SPO Roles:** Advance initiatives like the "icebreakers"
    program to incentivize SPOs and further decentralize infrastructure by
    enabling peer-to-peer transactions and allowing SPOs to determine their
    participation and earnings. Encourage SPOs to diversify their roles beyond L1 block
    production, including operating Hydra nodes, Midgard validators, oracles
    (e.g., Charlie 3), or hosting services.
* **SPO Registry and Incentives:** Explore an SPO registry to list operators
    capable of supporting Layer 2 protocols (Hydra) or Actively Validated Services
    (AVS) for partner chains, informing future incentive mechanisms. Assess traditional SPO incentives, including minimum margin parameters
    and pledge-benefit curve (`a0`) modifications, to optimize network health
    and resilience.
* **Sustainable L2 Expansion:** Ensure L2 solutions provide value back to the
    main chain (L1) through mechanisms like batching fees in ADA, preventing
    "leeching" and promoting sustainable growth of the broader Cardano
    ecosystem.

#### 6. Advancing Core Protocol Capabilities

* **L1 Scalability and Finality:** Continue development of L1 enhancements
    like Leios for increased transaction throughput through parallel block
    creation and Peras for accelerated transaction settlement via SPO voting.
    Address trade-offs such as increased complexity in consensus
    and network traffic, ensuring robust infrastructure for high transaction
    volumes.
* **L2 Solutions for Application Scaling:** Further develop and integrate Hydra
    and Midgard as critical frameworks for enabling applications to scale
    effectively off-chain, supporting high-throughput and low-cost transactions
    for specific use cases.
* **Enhanced Transaction Models:** Implement intent-based transactions (nested
    transactions) for greater efficiency and security, including "batcher-less"
    DEXs and improved user onboarding via Babel Fees. Babel Fees will allow users
    to pay transaction fees using non-ADA native assets, lowering the barrier to
    entry. Address increased transaction construction complexity
    and the need for robust tooling. A split mempool is crucial to prevent nested
    transactions from clogging the network.

### Operational Principles

* **Capital Efficiency:** Narrow focus to accelerate development and learning;
    pivot quickly if initial approaches are ineffective.
* **Alignment:** Compound efforts through focused work, amplifying execution
    strength.
* **Differentiated Strategy:** Achieve specificity to create defensibility;
    strive to be best-in-class in chosen areas.
* **Inclusiveness through Clarity:** A clear vision galvanizes the committed
    and provides unambiguous direction.

### Strategic Metrics

Success will be measured by:

* **Annual L1 Transaction Fees:** Tracking progress toward the $200 million
    target.
* **Enterprise Integrations:** Number of major corporations and financial
    institutions utilizing Cardano for specific use cases.
* **Developer Engagement:** Growth in active builders and contributions to
    community-led documentation and tooling.
* **Localized Adoption:** Measurable impact in targeted "low-hanging fruit"
    markets and regions, reflecting culturally intelligent growth strategies.
* **Network Decentralization & Resilience:** Metrics reflecting the
    diversification of SPO roles and the robustness of L2 solutions feeding
    value back to L1.

**Considerations from Business Engagements:**

A critical factor for widespread enterprise adoption of public blockchains
is addressing the perception of application-level security risk, which is
currently a non-starter for major corporations. Companies often distinguish
between protocol-level and application-level security, and the latter on
public blockchains presents significant challenges for them. Solutions
that provide a secure environment for large corporations to interact with
public blockchains are essential, requiring real-time and AI-driven
detection and prevention mechanisms that can intercept interactions with
wallets and validators.

For public companies, managing treasury and invoicing on a blockchain can
also present unusual business challenges. While banks are exploring
stablecoins and real-world asset tokenization, their interactions for
liquidity use cases often lean towards private blockchains due to
cybersecurity concerns. Overcoming these security and operational hurdles
is crucial for broadening Cardano's appeal to Fortune 500 companies and
mainstream financial institutions.
